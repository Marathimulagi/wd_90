Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj


Battel of Pratapgad


On November 10, 1659, Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj famously defeated and killed Afzal Khan, a powerful Adilshahi general, near Pratapgad Fort. Anticipating treachery during their meeting, Shivaji Maharaj wore hidden armor and used tiger claws (wagh nakh) to kill Khan, a pivotal moment that established Maratha power in the Deccan.

Key Details of the Conflict: The Campaign: In 1659, Afzal Khan was sent by the Bijapur Sultanate to crush the rising power of Shivaji Maharaj. Khan brought a massive, well-equipped army and was known for his strength and ruthless methods, having previously destroyed temples and harmed Maratha interests. The Strategy: Recognizing he was outmatched in open warfare, Shivaji Maharaj lured Khan to the dense forests of Pratapgad, forcing him to bring a smaller force. The Meeting (November 10, 1659): The two met in a tent. Khan embraced Shivaji Maharaj and tried to stab him in the back. Thanks to hidden armor, Shivaji survived and used his "wagh nakh" (tiger claws) to disembowel Khan.

Aftermath: Following the killing, the Maratha army, which was strategically positioned, attacked and defeated the leaderless Bijapur army. This victory significantly boosted the strength and morale of the fledgling Maratha empire.

Significance: This event is considered a masterclass in diplomacy and guerrilla warfare. It proved Shivaji Maharaj's capability to defeat larger forces and marked his emergence as a formidable ruler against the Deccan sultanates.
